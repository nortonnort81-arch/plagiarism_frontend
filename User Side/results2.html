Accurate detection and identification of pharmaceutical pills are crucial to maintaining patient safety, ensuring effective clinical treatment, and meeting regulatory standards in contemporary healthcare systems and pharmacies. The traditional manual approach used in pharmacies and hospitals has several limitations. These methods, although capable of correctly interpreting electronic prescriptions and spotting counterfeit medications, are vulnerable to human error, particularly when delivered in highly stressful contexts. To overcome these limitations, recent advances in artificial intelligence and machine learning have enabled the development of sophisticated pill recognition systems using digital imaging systems. Traditional approaches rely on visual inspection and manual analyses of sample matching techniques, comparing extracted features from pill images with previously stored reference images. However, methods that rely on shape geometries, variable lighting, and diverse backgrounds have several limitations. For instance, convolutional neural networks trained on deep learning-based solutions for pill classification and recognition have been applied, although they have computational complexity poses limitations for real-time applications. Furthermore, AI-driven solutions have proven their performance in detecting standard pill types such as circular and oval tablets, but these systems still have limitations when identifying irregular or manually broken pills, often complicated by the presence of pills with similar appearances but different pharmacokinetics and compounds. Additionally, real-time recognition using webcams or mobile devices introduces technical challenges such as varying light conditions, occlusions, background noise, and inconsistent lighting conditions.
